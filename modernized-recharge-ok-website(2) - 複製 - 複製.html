RECHARGE-OK: Revitalizing Oklahoma with Electric Energy

Recharge-OK is an innovative project designed to enhance Oklahoma's energy resilience through the integration of Electric School Buses (ESB) with Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) technology, promoting sustainable energy use.

NSF Award Number: 2431225

Technology Explanation

What is V2G Technology?

Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) technology allows electric vehicles to return power to the grid, providing backup power and enhancing grid stability. This makes electric school buses perfect candidates for pilot V2G initiatives due to their predictable schedules and large battery capacity.

V2G technology works by connecting the vehicle's battery to the grid when it is not in use, allowing for energy to be drawn or provided based on the grid's needs. The ability to use stored energy from multiple vehicles can reduce the reliance on traditional power plants, especially during peak demand times, and stabilize the overall power system. Electric school buses, with their consistent schedules, are an ideal platform for this pilot because they provide a reliable energy storage solution when not transporting students.

Partnerships & Community Engagement

Civic Partnerships

We collaborate with multiple community partners to make this project a success:

  • OUHSC (University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center): Provides health impact assessment and public health support for the community.
  • OG&E (Oklahoma Gas & Electric): Supplies expertise in energy management and grid integration for the successful implementation of V2G technology.
  • Shawnee Public Schools: Participates in pilot testing by providing electric school buses for the project, ensuring real-world testing and community involvement.
  • City of Oklahoma City: Supports regulatory compliance and policy alignment to help facilitate the integration of electric school buses into the city's public transportation system.

Project Stages

The project is divided into multiple stages to ensure efficient implementation:

  • Stage 1: Community and partnership building, kick-off events, and defining research questions.
  • Stage 2: Proposal writing, pilot implementation, and scalability evaluation.

Impact & Outcomes

Technological and Social Impact

Our project aims to enhance grid resilience, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and engage local communities through active participation.

By integrating V2G technology into school buses, the project provides a cleaner, more efficient energy solution. It also contributes to the reliability and stability of the grid by using stored energy to meet peak demands. Furthermore, this project fosters educational opportunities for students and community members to learn about sustainability and renewable energy.
